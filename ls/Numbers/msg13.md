lighting the lamps and presenting the levites for the service.

the lamps of the lampstand shone to enlighten to holy place. this (the seven fold
intensified Spirit) was the light by which the priests could do their service.

the levites were cleansed by:
- the water of purification (for sins)
- the shaving of all their flesh (the cross to not depend on our natural strength)
- washing their clothes (their conduct) and themselves
- offering a sin offering, burnt offering and meal offering

identified (replacing) all the children of israel in their service to God. presented
as a living wave offering (resurrection) to the lord.

at 25 the levites could start to serve and at 50 they had to stop. after 50 they were
still useful to instruct the younger ones concerning all the laws of the sanctuary,
that they wouldn't make a mistake and suffer loss.

> The point here is that there was the need of some experiences ones to instruct
> God's people so that they would not do anything wrong to offend Him.

> Here in Numbers we have a full type of tbe church service. I hope that the Lord
> would open our eyes to see that, comparatively speaking, in the church service we have
> been somehwat loose. With respect to spiritual principles, we have not been so
> serious, strict and right. Our making mistakes unconsciously could be the reason
> that we have missed much of God's blessing. In serving God, we should not be loose
> but should be serious. We all, especially the young ones, need to learn the spiritual
> regulations and spiritual laws regarding the service of God.
