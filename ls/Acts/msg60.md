Paul gave his defence to the Jews after being arrested by the Romans. in this
defence he spoke about his conversion, but it was when he mentioned being sent away
to the Gentiles by tbe Lord, that the Jews could not stand it anymore. this was a
matter of the dispensational transfer amd it seemed as if as soon as he spoke about
this word a whirlwind came in to stir up the people.

Paul was sovereignly kept by the Lord. the Lord kept him from completing the
nazarite vow, by stirring up the jews. the Lord kept him from the rioting jews
by the intervention of the roman commander. paul also used his wisdom to avoid
suffering by utilizing his roman citizenship. after this the Lord protected
him from the plot of the jews by sending hundreds of roman soliders to protect him
as he delivered him to the governer. during this time paul was protected for two years
so that he could take time to consider the matters he would write in his last four
books, Hebrews, Ephesians, Pbilippians and Colossians.
