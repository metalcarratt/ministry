both Peter and John had the experience on the mount where they saw Jesus' glory
and beheld Him talking with Moses and Elijah. Both of them also heard the Father's
strong declaration to hear only the Son, and beheld "Jesus alone". From this they
should have been clear the the entire Old Testament dispensation, including the law
(Moses) and the prophets (Elijah) had been replaced by Christ.

after the Lord's resurrection amd before His ascension there are three emphatic
charges:
- Matt 28:19-20a, "go therefore and disciple all the nations"
- Mark 16:15, "go into all the world and preach the gospel to all creation"
- Luke 24:47, "to all the nations beginning from Jerusalem"

all three of Peter, John and James should have been clear concerning this. what
James said about thousands zealous for the law was a shame to him. why did Peter
and John say nothing? Even Paul said Peter was involved in hyprocrisy.
