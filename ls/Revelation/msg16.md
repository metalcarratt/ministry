laodecia

the speaker:
- the amen (firm, steadfaat, trustworthy)
- the faithful and true witness
- the beginning of the creation (the source of God's work)

the church:
- neither hot nor cols but lukewarm
- proud of being rich
- wretched, miserable, poor, blind, naked
- about to be vommited out of the Lord's mouth
- the Lord standing at the door and knocking

buying gold refined by fire. gold signifies the divine nature of God, and the faith through which we can participate in the divine nature. in order to gain this we need to pay a price. if we ask the Lord for this living faith He may ask us to drop something. that is the price. if we are willing to drop it we will gain the gold. Paul said He counted all things loss that He might gain Christ.

a white garment to clothe our nakedness. this is Christ lived out of us. if we have the living faith to participate in the divine nature, this nature will eventually come out of us as our living. if we do not love the Lord and live by, for and with Him, we will be naked. our experience confirms this. but if we love the Lord and live Him He will become our beautiful covering. we will realise this in our spirit, and it will not be a surprise when the Lord comes to take you to fight with Him against antichrist.


in ephesus is the eating of the tree of life, in pergamos the earing of the hidden manna, and in Laodecia dining with the Lord as typified by the all-inclusive good land.
