# Message Two - The Ministry of Reconciliation

The ministry of reconciliation - is this the peace offering?

- Rom. 5:10, "For if **we, being enemies**, **were reconciled to God through the death** of His Son, **much more** we will be **saved in His life**, having been reconciled,"
  - His was a death of reconciliation
- Col. 1:20, "And through Him to reconcile all things to Himself, having made peace **through the blood of His cross** — through Him, whether the things on the earth or the things in the heavens."
  - God is the One who initiated this reconciliation
- 2 Cor. 5:18-20
  - v18, But all things are out from God, who has reconciled us to Himself through Christ and has given to us the ministry of reconciliation;
  - v19, Namely, that God in Christ was reconciling the world to Himself, not accounting their offenses to them, and has put in us the word of reconciliation.
  - v20, On behalf of Christ then we are ambassadors, as God entreats you through us; we beseech you on behalf of Christ, **Be reconciled to God**.

- We all *have* been reconciled but there is still a need for a "much more" reconciliation.
- The fruit of life is righteousness

### Basic understanding

Memorise these 8 facts
1. The *need* - enmity, enemies
2. The *meaning* - to make peace - deliver me from being a "troublemaker"; make me a "peacemaker"
3. The *instrument* - Christ
4. the *means* - the cross - the cross not only terminates but germinates
5. the "scope* - all things
6. the *sphere* - in one Body - the place where there is peace, where we are being reconciled
7. the *fact* - our position before God
8. the *experience* - in one Spirit, access unto the Father



- The apostles were authorized by Christ to represent Him. God is the unique reconciler, but He needs our help.
- the church in Corinth was a genuine church with saints who had the initial reconciliation. But they had a further need; "I heard there are divisions among you."
- how much we need to be reconciled (do we think we still do not have emnity with God?)
  - James, "friendship with **the world** is emnity against God"
  - Romans, "the mind set on **the flesh** is emnity against God"
- need to be identified with the crucified Christ - stay on the cross
- the ministry of reconciliation brings believers: absolutely **into** God and makes them absolutely **one** with Him.
  - united, mingled, incorporated - this is the result of reconciliation
- The *veil* of the *flesh*, the natural man, *separates* them from God
  - Paul was working to *cut asunder* the *separating veil* of the flesh
  - The flesh is keeping us away from God. he flesh is the obstacle. The flesh is the blockage to our experience of God in the Holy of Holies
- The *extent* to which we can bring others to God is measured by *where we are* with respect to God
  - outer court -> Holy place > Holy of Holies
